# Lisbon Events — Research Brief

You are a meticulous local-knowledge researcher. Build the single most COMPLETE directory of places and sources for events in Lisbon, Portugal. Completeness is the goal: include obscure, niche, alternative, DIY and hyper-local spots, not just the famous ones. Cover both Portuguese-language and English/expat-facing sources. Use current (2026) information and note anything seasonal.

## Output format

For EVERY entry, give:
- Name
- Neighbourhood / area
- One line on what events it hosts or announces
- Website URL and/or Instagram @handle

## Rules

- Organise the output under the category headings below, as bulleted lists.
- Prioritise breadth and specificity over brevity. Long is expected.
- Deduplicate across categories.
- Flag any place that may have closed, since independent and DIY venues turn over fast.
- The examples in each category are starting points, NOT limits. Find many more in each.

## Categories to exhaust

## Discovery channels (use these, not just websites and Instagram)

- Facebook Events and Facebook Groups: Lisbon Expats, Girls Gone International Lisbon, InterNations Lisbon, digital-nomad and neighbourhood groups.
- Telegram and WhatsApp channels and Communities: party crews, run clubs, hobby and neighbourhood groups.
- Reddit: r/lisbon and r/portugal event threads.
- Newsletters and Substacks: personal "what's on in Lisbon" curators, plus individual venue mailing lists.
- Meetup.com and Luma (lu.ma): recurring community, tech and design events.
- Ticketing and experience platforms: Eventbrite, Fever, Fatsoma, Xceed, GetYourGuide, Airbnb Experiences.
- Gig and club apps: DICE, Bandsintown, Songkick, Resident Advisor, Shotgun.
- TikTok and YouTube creators who cover Lisbon events and nightlife.
- LinkedIn Events for professional, tech and design networking.
- Erasmus Student Network (ESN) Lisboa and university student-union pages.
- Couchsurfing Hangouts and events; social and dinner apps like Timeleft and Bumble BFF.
- Strava clubs for run and cycle event calendars.
- Record shops as scene info-hubs, plus their socials and boards: Flur, Carpet & Snares, Groovie, Louie Louie.
- Independent and community radio that plug the scene, e.g. Rádio Quântica.
- Discord servers for board-game, tech, queer and hobby communities.
- Press culture sections: Público (Ípsilon and Cinecartaz), Observador, Expresso, Time Out print, NiT.
- Embassy and consulate cultural calendars; venue Patreon and membership feeds.
- Physical sources: posters and flyers in Bairro Alto and Cais do Sodré, café and venue noticeboards, library and cultural-centre boards, tourism kiosks (Ask Me Lisboa).
- Google "Things to do" and Google Maps events.

## Method

Search broadly and repeatedly. Go neighbourhood by neighbourhood: Santos, Cais do Sodré, Bairro Alto, Chiado, Príncipe Real, Intendente, Anjos, Arroios, Graça, Alfama, Marvila, Beato, Alcântara, Belém, Campo de Ourique, Avenidas Novas, Alvalade. Mine each aggregator and Instagram account for the venues they repeatedly feature, then follow links to find more. Do not stop at the well-known names. Chase the long tail.
