# Batch 03 — Live music, annual festivals & sacred music

Coverage note: entries verified against current (2026) sources where possible. Venues that closed or moved are flagged. Cross-category overlaps (nightlife = cat. 3; DIY/cultural spaces = cat. 9) are noted but kept here when they have substantial live-music programming.

## 10. Live music & concert venues (all sizes) + fado houses

### Large halls, arenas & mid-size concert venues

- **MEO Arena (formerly Altice Arena / Pavilhão Atlântico)** — Parque das Nações — Portugal's largest indoor arena (~20,000 cap); the biggest international tours (2026: Electric Callboy, Hans Zimmer, Rosalía, Doja Cat, Eros Ramazzotti, Ne-Yo). Website: arena.meo.pt — Instagram: @meoarena
- **Coliseu dos Recreios** — Baixa / Restauradores — Historic ~4,000-cap domed hall; pop, classical, comedy, family shows (2026: Ludovico Einaudi, MARO, José González, NAPA). Website: coliseulisboa.com — Instagram: @coliseulisboa
- **Campo Pequeno** — Avenidas Novas — Converted neo-Moorish bullring used for rock/metal/pop concerts and trade fairs (2026: Biffy Clyro, Alter Bridge, Suede, The Kooks). Website: campopequeno.com — Instagram: @campopequenooficial
- **Capitólio (Vodafone Capitólio)** — Parque Mayer (off Av. da Liberdade) — Restored modernist hall, ~600–1,000 cap; indie, electronic, alt-pop gigs and showcases; a Vodafone Mexefest venue. Website: capitolio.pt — Instagram: @capitolio.lisboa
- **Aula Magna** — Cidade Universitária (Avenidas Novas / Alvalade) — University of Lisbon auditorium; seated concerts (2026: The Divine Comedy, Ryan Adams) and the Reitoria recital series. Website: aulamagna.pt — Instagram: @aulamagna_ul
- **LAV – Lisboa ao Vivo** — Alcântara (near Doca de Alcântara) — Mid-size standing venue (~1,500); touring international and Portuguese acts across rock, electronic, hip-hop; also a jazz programming spot. Website: lisboaaovivo.pt — Instagram: @lisboaaovivo
- **Cinema São Jorge** — Avenida da Liberdade — Cinema/auditorium that doubles as a concert and festival venue (hosts Vodafone Mexefest, festival nights). Website: cinemasaojorge.pt — Instagram: @cinemasaojorge
- **Pavilhão Carlos Lopes** — Parque Eduardo VII (Avenidas Novas) — Restored exhibition pavilion used for mid-large concerts, festivals (was the Sónar Lisboa home) and fairs. Website: pavilhaocarloslopes.pt — Instagram: @pavilhaocarloslopes

### Iconic club-venues with live programming

- **Lux Frágil** — Santa Apolónia / Cais da Pedra (riverside, near Alfama) — Portugal's most famous club (since 1998); live shows plus house/techno/disco nights and international DJs across two floors + terrace. Website: luxfragil.com — Instagram: @luxfragil
- **Casa Capitão** — Beato (Hub Criativo do Beato / Beato Innovation District) — The successor to Musicbox: the Musicbox team and programming relocated here in Sept 2025; multidisciplinary venue with concerts, DJ sets, exhibitions, markets, performances and workshops (2026 acts: Bonga, Greentea Peng, Letrux, Saul Williams, Water From Your Eyes, Wavves). Website: casa-capitao.com — Instagram: @casacapitao
- **Musicbox Lisboa** — Cais do Sodré (under the arches off Pink Street) — **[CLOSED]** shut 15 Sept 2025 after 19 years owing to redevelopment of Cais do Sodré; team and programming moved to Casa Capitão (Beato). Listed for reference only.
- **B.Leza** — Cais do Sodré (riverside, Cais da Ribeira Nova) — The home of African and Lusophone music in Lisbon; live bands Thu–Sun (kizomba, semba, morna, Afrobeat, salsa) plus dance nights. Instagram: @bleza_oficial — Facebook: B.Leza
- **Titanic Sur Mer** — Cais do Sodré (under the arcades, Av. Ribeira das Naus) — Daytime terrace that becomes a club; jazz/samba concerts, electronic nights, cabaret, samba classes and workshops. Website: titanicsurmer.com — Instagram: @titanicsurmer
- **RCA Club** — Alvalade — Lisbon's main rock/metal/hardcore/punk club since 2013; live bands Fri/Sat (past acts: Angra, Sick of It All, Madball, Anti-Flag). Website: rcaclub.com — Instagram: @rcaclublx
- **ZDB – Galeria Zé dos Bois** — Bairro Alto (Rua da Barroca) — Independent art association since 1994; ~150 events/year of indie, experimental, electronic and contemporary music plus dance/performance. Website: zedosbois.org — Instagram: @galeriazedosbois
- **Sabotage Club** — Cais do Sodré (Rua de São Paulo 16) — Small alternative club; local and touring rock, indie, punk, garage bands; relocated within Cais do Sodré after leaving its original Pink Street spot. Website: sabotage.pt — Instagram: @sabotagerock
- **Damas** — Graça (Rua da Voz do Operário) — Restaurant-bar-concert space; alternative jazz, ambient, electroacoustic, grunge, synth-pop most nights. Instagram: @damaslisboa
- **8 Marvila** — Marvila — Large industrial-warehouse club mainly for electronic acts but also live headline shows (2026: Fatboy Slim). Mostly cat. 3 nightlife. Instagram: @8marvila
- **Village Underground Lisboa** — Alcântara (near Museu da Carris) — Creative hub built from shipping containers and buses; hosts gigs, DJ nights, talks and showcases. Website: vulisboa.com — Instagram: @villageunderground.lisboa
- **Cabaret Maxime** — Praça da Alegria (off Av. da Liberdade) — Former cabaret/club that programmes alternative gigs, burlesque, electronic and rock nights. Instagram: @cabaretmaxime
- **Plateau / Tokyo / Jamaica / Europa** — Santos / Avenida 24 de Julho — Long-standing nightclubs on the 24 de Julho strip with occasional live sets; mostly cat. 3 nightlife. (Plateau: @plateauclub; Tokyo: @tokyo.lisboa; Jamaica: @discotecajamaica)

### Jazz & art-music venues

- **Hot Clube de Portugal** — Praça da Alegria (off Av. da Liberdade) — One of Europe's oldest jazz clubs (est. 1948), rated among the world's best by DownBeat; live jazz Tue–Sat, sets ~10.30pm/11.30pm; runs a jazz school and partners on Santa Casa jazz programming. Website: hcp.pt — Instagram: @hotclubeportugal
- **Páginas Tantas** — Bairro Alto (Rua do Diário de Notícias 85) — Long-running intimate jazz bar with live sets and jam nights; emerging and established players. Instagram: @paginastantasjazzbar
- **O'culto da Ajuda – art music centre** — Ajuda (Travessa das Zebras 25) — Miso Music Portugal's programming space (since 2014); contemporary/experimental/improvised music, jazz, sound art, poetry and performance. Website: misomusic.me — Instagram: @oculto_da_ajuda
- **Casa Cheia – Espaço de Pesquisa Artística** — Penha de França / Alto de São João (Rua Lopes 3) — Intimate artistic-research hall run by Alice Ruiz & Miguel Mateus; concerts across many genres, residencies, music/theatre classes. Website: casacheia.pt — Instagram: @casacheia.lisboa
- **Cossoul** — Madragoa / Santos (Rua Nova da Piedade 66) — Cultural association running monthly jazz jam sessions with the Conservatório / Lusíada jazz course; student + guest musicians. Website: cossoul.pt — Instagram: @cossoul_associacao
- **Espaço Lisboa Jazz** — Alcântara (top floor of the Espaço Lisboa restaurant) — Intimate jazz bar exploring blues, fusion and beyond. Instagram: @espacolisboarestaurante
- **Bus – Paragem Cultural** — Anjos / Arroios (Rua de São Lázaro area) — Small bar-venue with jazz sessions, singer-songwriters, Latin, Afrobeat, funk and cumbia bands. Instagram: @bus.paragemcultural

## 25. Classical & sacred music in churches

- **Igreja de São Roque – Temporada Música em São Roque** — Bairro Alto / Chiado (Largo Trindade Coelho) — Organised by Santa Casa da Misericórdia (since 1988), one of Lisbon's oldest concert seasons; orchestras, choirs and early/sacred music; recent openings include the Orquestra Metropolitana de Lisboa (Händel/Telemann) and Orquestra Sem Fronteiras (Vivaldi/Bach). Performed under the 1784 pipe organ; some concerts also at Igreja de São Pedro de Alcântara and Casa Ásia. Website: tmsr.scml.pt — Instagram: @santacasalisboa
- **Igreja de São Vicente de Fora – Ciclo de Concertos de Órgão** — Alfama / Graça (Largo de São Vicente) — Organ-concert cycle (artistic direction João Vaz) on the second Saturday of the month at 5pm, roughly April–December; free entry via the main door; historic João Fontanes de Maqueira organ. Also hosts the "Concerto de São Vicente – Ano Novo" (January) with Capella Patriarchal. Info via Althum (althumeditora.com) — Website: mosteirodesaovicentedefora.com — Facebook: Órgão de São Vicente de Fora (seasonal: spring–autumn + New Year).
- **Sé de Lisboa (Lisbon Cathedral)** — Alfama / Sé — Occasional sacred concerts and the Cathedral Choir; primarily a monument visit but used for special liturgical/choral concerts. Website: sedelisboa.pt
- **Basílica da Estrela** — Estrela / Campo de Ourique — Late-Baroque/neoclassical basilica with a historic 1791 António Xavier Machado e Cerveira organ (restored 1998); hosts occasional sacred-music, organ and choral concerts, including the Pequenos Cantores da Basílica da Estrela choir. (Verify dates via parish; Patriarcado de Lisboa calendar: patriarcado-lisboa.pt.)
- **Mosteiro dos Jerónimos – Ciclo de Concertos de Órgão** — Belém — Organ-concert cycle organised by ACMJ (Associação Cultura e Música nos Jerónimos) in the Manueline monastery church; plus choral and commemorative concerts. Website: mosteirojeronimos.gov.pt — Facebook: acmjeronimos
- **Centro Nacional de Cultura – "Concertos de Órgão"** — various Lisbon churches — CNC-organised historic-organ recital series in churches across the city. Website: cnc.pt
- **Lisbon Classical Nights (LX Classical Nights)** — Cais do Sodré (Sacristy of Igreja de São Paulo) — Intimate classical-guitar recitals Mon–Sat at 7pm, ~50 min, ~20–25 guests. Website: lisbonclassicalnights.com — Instagram: @lisbonclassicalnights
- **Igreja de Santa Engrácia / Panteão Nacional** — Alfama (Campo de Santa Clara) — National Pantheon, occasionally used for concerts and commemorative musical events under its dome. Website: panteaonacional.gov.pt
- **Igreja / Convento do Carmo (Museu Arqueológico do Carmo)** — Chiado (Largo do Carmo) — The roofless Carmo ruins host open-air concerts and seasonal music events in the nave (mostly summer). Website: museuarqueologicodocarmo.pt — Instagram: @museuarqueologicodocarmo (seasonal: summer)
- **Igreja de São Domingos** — Baixa (Largo de São Domingos) — Large downtown church occasionally used for sacred-music and choral concerts.
- **Igreja de São Luís dos Franceses** — Chiado / Bairro Alto — French national church that occasionally programmes sacred concerts (often via the French community / Alliance Française).
- **Serviço de Música Sacra da Igreja de São Roque (SMSISRoque)** — Bairro Alto / Chiado — Dedicated sacred-music service of the Irmandade de São Roque, running liturgical and concert programming distinct from the Temporada. Facebook: ServicoMusicaSacraIgrejaSaoRoque — Website: irmandadesaoroque.pt
- **Free Christmas concerts in Lisbon churches** — various churches citywide — Annual programme of free Christmas/sacred concerts (e.g. "Um Natal Austríaco" and parish-organised choirs/organ) in December, listed via AgendaLX and the Pastoral da Cultura. (Seasonal: December; also Easter choral repertoire.)

### Seasonality note

Church/organ cycles cluster in spring–summer (São Vicente de Fora cycle ~April–December on second Saturdays; Festival ao Largo and Jazz em Agosto in July–August) and around Christmas/Easter for choral and sacred repertoire. The São Roque season runs across the cultural year (autumn–spring). Out Jazz is a May–September Sunday series in city parks. Festival dates are confirmed where stated above; Vodafone Mexefest 2026 and Santa Casa Jazz Fest 2026 remain to verify, and Sónar Lisboa skips 2026. Always confirm exact 2026 dates on each venue's site, as church concerts are frequently one-off and announced close to the date.

### Cross-category notes

Lux, B.Leza, Titanic Sur Mer, 8 Marvila and the 24 de Julho clubs (Plateau/Tokyo/Jamaica/Europa) also sit under nightlife (cat. 3); ZDB, Anjos70, Sirigaita, Disgraça, Crew Hassan, Arroz Estúdios, Fábrica Braço de Prata, Casa Independente, Village Underground and MOB overlap with DIY/cultural spaces (cat. 8/9). Ageas Cooljazz (Cascais) and OUT.FEST (Barreiro) sit in greater Lisbon. Casa Capitão (Beato) is the live-music successor to the closed Musicbox.
